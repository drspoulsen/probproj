Section 1.1 The Multiplication Principle

Activity 1.1.1.
  1. Suppose that you just moved to a new country and brought a limited wardrobe of three shirts and four pants. How many outfits (pairings of shirts and pants) can you form? To see the answer, lay out all the possible outfits in a grid (you might need to label your shirts and pants, feel free to be creative!)
  2. If you had 100 shirts and 20 pants, how many outfits could you form? Would you want to list out all the possible outfits?
