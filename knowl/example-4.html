Example 1.1.2.

You just bought a new lock for your locker. It requires 4 characters. However, the first two are letters and the second two are digits. How many different password combinations are there? What if no repeats are allowed?
Solution.
\((26)(26)(10)(10) = 67600\) possible password combinations.
\((26)(25)(10)(9) = 58500\) possible password combinations.
